**AI-Driven Meta-Learning for Federated Systems**  
*Overview*:  
AI-driven meta-learning for federated systems combines **meta-learning** (learning to learn) with **federated learning** (a decentralized machine learning approach). This synergy enables models to adapt quickly to new tasks while maintaining data privacy, as training occurs across distributed devices without sharing raw data. Key applications include healthcare, IoT, and edge computing.  

*Key Developments*:  
1. **Efficient Model Personalization**: Meta-learning techniques (e.g., Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning, MAML) are used to optimize federated models for heterogeneous client data, reducing communication costs.  
2. **Privacy-Preserving Techniques**: Federated meta-learning integrates differential privacy and secure aggregation to protect sensitive data during distributed training.  
3. **Scalability Improvements**: Research focuses on reducing computational overhead in federated settings, such as using lightweight meta-learners for edge devices.  

*Challenges*:  
- **Data Heterogeneity**: Clients may have non-IID (non-independent and identically distributed) data, complicating model generalization.  
- **Security Risks**: Vulnerabilities like model inversion attacks require robust encryption and validation mechanisms.  
- **Communication Efficiency**: Balancing frequent model updates with bandwidth constraints remains a critical hurdle.
